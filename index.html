About MapleLeaf Retreats

A little review desk with a very stubborn independence

MapleLeaf Retreats started in 2013 as a newsletter shared between friends and grew into one of the country's most-trusted guides to the Canadian stays actually worth the trip. We're not a booking engine, and no hotel chain owns a word of us.

Our writers pay their own way, keep their names off the reservation, and report on what really happens between check-in and check-out — the welcome, the quiet, the frayed edges nobody photographs. We never publish prices, we never trade a kind word for a commission, and every review carries the name of the person who slept there.

If a link on this site leads you to book a room, the property may pay us a small commission. That keeps our reviews free — and it has never once bought a better score.

Our method

What goes into a MapleLeaf review

The same unhurried, four-step routine sits behind every rating you'll read here.

01

We book anonymously

Rooms are reserved and paid for like any other guest's — no comps, no press rate, and no heads-up to the front desk.

02

We settle in

Our writer spends at least one full night testing the bed, the water pressure, the walls and the way the staff treat you on day two.

03

We listen widely

We read hundreds of recent guest reviews so a single dreamy (or dreadful) night never tips the scales on its own.

04

We say it plainly

A signed, first-person review with a clear verdict — full marks where they're earned, and honest words where they aren't.
