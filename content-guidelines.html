Content Guidelines & Reporting

The standards we hold ourselves to — and how to flag content you believe crosses a line.

Last updated: 1 July 2026

Our editorial standards

Every review and guide on MapleLeaf Retreats is expected to be:

  • Accurate. Facts about a hotel — location, facilities, contact details — are checked against reliable sources at the time of writing.
  • Independent. Verdicts reflect our writers' genuine experience and real guest sentiment, free from commercial influence.
  • Fair. We praise what deserves praise and criticise what deserves criticism, and we give context rather than cherry-picking.
  • Respectful. We do not publish content that is hateful, harassing, defamatory or discriminatory.
  • Transparent. Commercial relationships are disclosed, and updates are dated.

How we handle guest reviews

The traveller reviews shown on our hotel pages are drawn from publicly available guest feedback and are presented to reflect the balance of real opinion — positive and negative. We condense for length and clarity, and we do not fabricate praise. We remove content that appears fake, defamatory, or that reveals private personal information.

Reporting content

If you believe something on this site is inaccurate, out of date, unfair, or otherwise breaches these guidelines, please tell us. This includes hotels wishing to correct factual errors about their property.

To report content, email tom.mercier.133@gmail.com or use our feedback form (topic: "A hotel review"). Please include:
  • The page or review in question (a link is ideal);
  • What you believe is wrong or inappropriate;
  • Any supporting information, and how we can reach you.

What happens next

  1. We acknowledge your report, usually within two business days.
  2. Our editorial team reviews the content against these guidelines and the available evidence.
  3. Where a correction is warranted, we update the content and note the change.
  4. We let you know the outcome.

Intellectual property & takedowns

If you are a rights holder and believe your material has been used improperly (for example, a photograph), contact tom.mercier.133@gmail.com with details and we will respond promptly.
